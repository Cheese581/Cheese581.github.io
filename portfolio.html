Gameplay Background Music/Loops

Anything Can Happen Next - A loop inspired by the opening scene of an imaginary adventure or RPG game.
Groovy Jam??? - I think I used this for a game where you navigate a 3D map without losing water or stepping on lava, but I'm not sure. I sort of made it as its own thing because it sounded funny.
Ticking Time - Background music for an unfinished multiplayer trivia game. This would have played while creating and answering questions for one another, which you would only get so much time to complete.
Alien Annihilation - Background music for a game where a rat destroys a bunch of aliens.
Descent Into Darkness - A spooky ambient track written for a platformer game where you work with another player to escape a cave. The glockenspiel gets annoying in the middle, but that makes it more anxious and annoying so you really want to get out of there quick.

Murder Mystery Background Music

Blood on the Clocktower Introduction - The start of the story. This music underlied the horror monologue I gave when organizing a game of Blood on the Clocktower, a social deception/murder mystery game where a demon is on the loose.
Blodd on the Clocktower Rules - Background music for the murder mystery game while I explained all the rules. From 1:45ish onward, inspired by/arranged from Apex Predator from Mean Girls the Musical to prank someone.
