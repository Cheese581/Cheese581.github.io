Sweet, Sweet Betrayal Music Tracks

Sugar's On the Menu - This track plays in the main menu of Sweet, Sweet Betrayal.
Partytime - This track plays during the mad dash through the chaotic gauntlet as you race your friends (or enemies) to the sweet treasure that lies in store.
Sweet, Sweet Victory - This track plays for that highly skilled sugar glider who made it to the end with more treasure than the rest.
Holy Strawberry What's Taking the Other Sugar Gliders So Long To Get Here - This song is designed to be the elevator music of the sugar glider kingdom. After all, it can take a while for your friends to join a game room!

RabBeat Music Tracks (Jazz)

Bunny on the Run (Jazz) - This jazz track plays in the early levels of RabBeat as you run through a spooky lab full of your mutated friends who have turned against you.
Can't Stop the Groove (Jazz) - This is the intermediate jazz track from RabBeat. It plays as you groove through a world of disco mania, empowering you to never give up on saving your friends even in the face of deadly lasers.
Nocturne's Penthouse (Jazz) - This is the final jazz track from RabBeat. Within the evil villain's penthouse, only the most difficult of challenges remain as you rush to the end to exact revenge once and for all.
Rabbit Rave (Jazz) - This is an unused jazz track from RabBeat. It was used for initial playtesting but proved to be too fast for music-synced gameplay. It was later modified and fused into the main menu theme.

Additional Video Game Music Tracks

Chill GrubBug BGM - This is a background track for the magnificent game GrubBug. It ended up being unused as the game took a more energetic approach to its gameplay loop.
GrubBug Level Select Screen - This music is for the GrubBug level select screen. A short yet punchy one.
Candyland Adventure - This track was used during a summer game "jam" with part of the GrubBug team. It's meant to be light and loose.

Murder Mystery Background Music

Blood on the Clocktower Introduction - The start of the story. This music underlied the horror monologue I gave when organizing an in-person game of Blood on the Clocktower, a social deception/murder mystery game where a demon is on the loose. WARNING with headphones: it gets loud very suddenly around 2:28.
Blood on the Clocktower Rules - Background music for the murder mystery game while I explained all the rules. From 1:45ish onward, inspired by/arranged from Apex Predator from Mean Girls the Musical only because a bunch of the people playing had just been in that show.

Gameplay Background Music/Loops

Anything Can Happen Next - A loop inspired by the opening scene of an imaginary adventure or RPG game.
Bunny Demo Jazz - A quick burst of jazz used to test RabBeat's technical prototype.
Groovy Jam??? - I used this in a game where you navigate a 3D map without losing water or stepping on lava, but I sort of made it as its own thing just because.
Ticking Time - Background music for an unfinished multiplayer trivia game. This would have played while creating and answering questions for one another, which you would only get so much time to complete.
Alien Annihilation - Background music for a game where a rat destroys a bunch of aliens.
